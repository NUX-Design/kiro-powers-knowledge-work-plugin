# Knowledge Synthesis

Combine raw results from multiple systems into one coherent, deduplicated answer with clear attribution and confidence signaling.

## Deduplication

Merge results that represent the same underlying fact, decision, or document.

Signals include:

- same author or sender
- same project or entity
- closely matching text
- timestamps in the same window
- one source explicitly referencing another

Prefer the most complete, most authoritative, or most recent version when merging.

Keep items separate when:

- conclusions differ
- viewpoints differ
- the information evolved over time

## Attribution

Every important claim should be attributable to one or more sources.

Include:

- source category
- location such as channel, thread, folder, or title
- date
- author or sender when useful

Use both inline references and a source list when the answer benefits from traceability.

## Confidence

Assess confidence based on:

- freshness
- source authority
- agreement across sources

If confidence is low or information is stale, say so directly.

If sources conflict, surface the conflict and explain which source appears most current or authoritative.

## Summarization By Result Size

- small result set: provide direct details
- medium result set: group by theme
- large result set: synthesize top findings and offer drill-down

## Output Rules

- lead with the answer
- group by topic, not by source
- keep caveats and conflicts visible
- avoid raw source-by-source dumps
