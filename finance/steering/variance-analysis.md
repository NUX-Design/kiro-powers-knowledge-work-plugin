# Variance Analysis

Decompose financial variances into business drivers, narratives, and waterfall views.

This workflow assists with analysis and should be reviewed before inclusion in management or board materials.

## Decomposition Techniques

### Price / Volume

Use when a metric can be expressed as price multiplied by volume.

- volume effect
- price or rate effect
- mix effect when applicable

### Rate / Mix

Use for blended-rate analyses across segments with different economics.

### Headcount / Compensation

Separate variance into headcount, compensation rate, mix, timing, and attrition impacts.

### Spend Category

Break operating expense into:

- headcount-driven costs
- volume-driven costs
- discretionary spend
- fixed or contractual costs
- one-time items
- timing or phasing

## Materiality And Investigation

Set thresholds based on:

- organizational materiality
- line item size
- volatility
- management attention level

Prioritize investigation by:

1. largest dollar impact
2. largest percentage swing
3. unexpected direction
4. new variance
5. repeated trend

## Narrative Structure

```text
[Line Item]: [Favorable/Unfavorable] variance of $[amount] ([percentage]%)
vs [comparison basis] for [period]

Driver: [Primary driver description]
[2-3 concise sentences explaining the business reason]

Outlook: [One-time / Continuing / Improving / Deteriorating]
Action: [None / Monitor / Investigate / Update forecast]
```

## Waterfall Method

Use a bridge from baseline to actual with signed drivers that reconcile exactly to the ending value.

Keep:

- 5 to 8 drivers maximum
- smaller items grouped into `Other`
- ordering either by business logic or contribution size

## Output

Provide:

- the decomposition table
- variance narratives
- waterfall or bridge format
- follow-up questions or decisions required
