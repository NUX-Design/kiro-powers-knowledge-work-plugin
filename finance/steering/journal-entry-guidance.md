# Journal Entry Guidance

Reference best practices, standard entry types, documentation expectations, and approval controls for journal entries.

This material supports accounting operations but does not determine final accounting treatment without professional review.

## Standard Entry Types

### Accounts Payable Accruals

- Debit expense or capitalize when asset-qualifying.
- Credit accrued liabilities.
- Base estimates on PO receipts, contracts, recurring services, or submitted expenses.
- Reverse in the following period when appropriate.

### Fixed Asset Depreciation

- Debit depreciation or amortization expense.
- Credit accumulated depreciation or amortization.
- Validate useful life, method, additions, disposals, and impairments.

### Prepaid Amortization

- Debit the relevant expense category.
- Credit prepaid expense.
- Maintain start and end dates with an amortization schedule.

### Payroll Accruals

- Debit salary, bonus, benefits, and payroll tax expense.
- Credit accrued payroll, bonus, benefits, and payroll taxes.
- Include employer taxes, benefits, and PTO liabilities when required.

### Revenue Recognition

- Debit deferred revenue or accounts receivable depending on the event.
- Credit revenue.
- Follow contract-level obligations and transaction price allocation.

## Supporting Documentation Requirements

Every journal entry should include:

1. A clear memo explaining what is being recorded and why.
2. Calculation support showing formulas, schedules, or data references.
3. Source documents such as invoices, contracts, payroll registers, or PO references.
4. The accounting period covered.
5. Preparer identity and date.
6. Approval evidence based on the authorization matrix.
7. Reversal status and date when applicable.

## Typical Approval Matrix

| Entry Type | Amount Threshold | Approver |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| Standard recurring | Any amount | Accounting manager |
| Non-recurring / manual | < $50K | Accounting manager |
| Non-recurring / manual | $50K - $250K | Controller |
| Non-recurring / manual | > $250K | CFO / VP Finance |
| Top-side / consolidation | Any amount | Controller or above |
| Out-of-period adjustments | Any amount | Controller or above |

Adjust thresholds for your organization’s materiality and risk tolerance.

## Review Checklist

- [ ] Entry balances
- [ ] Period is open and correct
- [ ] Accounts and coding are appropriate
- [ ] Calculations and signs are correct
- [ ] Treatment aligns with policy and prior periods
- [ ] Reversal logic is appropriate
- [ ] Support is complete and referenced
- [ ] No duplicate or unexplained unusual entry exists

## Common Errors To Watch

1. Unbalanced entries
2. Wrong period posting
3. Reversed debit and credit signs
4. Duplicate entries
5. Wrong GL account
6. Missing reversal on accruals
7. Stale recurring estimates
8. Unsupported round-number estimates
9. Incorrect FX rates
10. Missing intercompany elimination
11. Capitalization mistakes
12. Period cut-off errors
