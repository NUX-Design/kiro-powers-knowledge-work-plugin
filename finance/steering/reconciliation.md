# Reconciliation

Reconcile accounts by comparing GL balances to subledgers, bank statements, or third-party data.

This workflow assists with reconciliation work and should be reviewed before sign-off.

## Reconciliation Types

### GL To Subledger

Compare a control account balance in the general ledger to the detailed subledger total.

Common areas:

- accounts receivable
- accounts payable
- fixed assets
- inventory
- prepaid expenses
- accrued liabilities

### Bank Reconciliation

Reconcile the GL cash balance to the bank statement using deposits in transit, outstanding checks, and unrecorded bank activity.

### Intercompany Reconciliation

Match reciprocal balances across related entities and confirm elimination logic for consolidation.

## Reconciling Item Categories

### Timing Differences

Items expected to clear in the normal cycle, such as outstanding checks, deposits in transit, or pending interface postings.

### Adjustments Required

Items that need a journal entry, such as unrecorded fees, interest, classification errors, or recording errors.

### Requires Investigation

Items that remain unexplained, have aged unexpectedly, or recur without resolution.

## Aging Framework

| Age Bucket | Status | Action |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| 0-30 days | Current | Monitor |
| 31-60 days | Aging | Investigate |
| 61-90 days | Overdue | Escalate |
| 90+ days | Stale | Management review |

## Escalation Thresholds

Define thresholds for:

- large individual items
- total unreconciled exposure
- aged items
- unexplained differences
- recurring trends

## Best Practices

1. Complete reconciliations within the close deadline.
2. Reconcile all material balance sheet accounts on a defined cadence.
3. Document every reconciling item clearly.
4. Maintain segregation of duties between processing and reconciliation.
5. Track items to final resolution instead of rolling them forever.

## Output

Produce:

- the reconciliation summary
- a list of reconciling items
- aging and categorization
- required adjusting entries
- escalation or follow-up recommendations
