# Meeting Briefing

Use this workflow to prepare a structured briefing for a meeting with legal relevance and to organize follow-up actions afterward.

Important: The output should be checked for accuracy before use in a live meeting.

## Step 1: Identify the Meeting

Determine:

- Title and meeting type
- Participants and roles
- Agenda or likely topics
- User’s role in the meeting
- Time available to prepare

## Step 2: Assess Preparation Needs

| Meeting Type | Typical Prep Needs |
|---|---|
| Deal review | Contract status, open issues, strategy, approvals |
| Board or committee | Legal updates, risks, pending matters, resolutions |
| Vendor call | Agreement status, issues, objectives, history |
| Team sync | Workload, deadlines, priorities |
| Customer or client | Agreement terms, open issues, relationship context |
| Regulatory | Matter history, compliance posture, counsel alignment |
| Litigation or dispute | Status, developments, strategy, settlement posture |
| Cross-functional | Legal implications, approvals, risk framing |

## Step 3: Gather Context

If tools are connected, gather context from:

- Calendar: meeting details, related meetings, attendee history
- Email: threads, prior follow-ups, shared documents
- Chat: team discussion, escalations, informal context
- Documents: agendas, notes, agreements, memos
- CLM: contract status and negotiation issues
- CRM: account context and stakeholder map

## Step 4: Build the Brief

```markdown
## Meeting Brief

### Meeting Details
- **Meeting**: [title]
- **Date/Time**: [with timezone]
- **Duration**: [duration]
- **Location**: [room or link]
- **Your Role**: [advisor / presenter / negotiator / observer]

### Participants
| Name | Organization | Role | Key Interests | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [name] | [org] | [role] | [interests] | [context] |

### Agenda / Expected Topics
1. [topic]
2. [topic]
3. [topic]

### Background and Context
[short synthesis]

### Key Documents
- [document] - [where to find it]

### Open Issues
| Issue | Status | Owner | Priority | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [issue] | [status] | [owner] | H / M / L | [context] |

### Legal Considerations
[specific legal implications]

### Talking Points
1. [point]
2. [point]
3. [point]

### Questions to Raise
- [question] - [why it matters]

### Decisions Needed
- [decision] - [options or recommendation]

### Red Lines / Non-Negotiables
[if applicable]

### Prior Meeting Follow-Up
[outstanding items]

### Preparation Gaps
[missing information]
```

## Meeting-Specific Additions

### Deal Reviews

Add:

- Deal summary
- Contract status
- Approval requirements
- Counterparty dynamics
- Comparable deal terms if available

### Board or Committee Meetings

Add:

- Legal department update
- Risk highlights
- Regulatory update
- Pending approvals
- Litigation summary

### Regulatory Meetings

Add:

- Regulator context
- History of prior interactions
- Current compliance posture
- Counsel coordination
- Privilege considerations

## Action Item Tracking

After the meeting, capture action items in this form:

```markdown
## Action Items from [Meeting Name] - [Date]

| # | Action Item | Owner | Deadline | Priority | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | [task] | [owner] | [date] | H / M / L | Open |
```

Best practices:

- Make actions specific
- Assign exactly one owner
- Use explicit deadlines
- Note dependencies
- Distinguish internal, external, and follow-up meeting actions where useful

## Follow-Up Rules

After the meeting:

1. Distribute action items.
2. Set reminders for deadlines.
3. Update relevant systems such as CLM, trackers, or risk registers.
4. File notes in the right repository.
5. Escalate urgent items immediately.

Tracking cadence:

- High priority: daily
- Medium priority: weekly or at the next sync
- Low priority: next scheduled review
- Overdue: escalate visibly
