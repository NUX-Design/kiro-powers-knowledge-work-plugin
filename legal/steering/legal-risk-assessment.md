# Legal Risk Assessment

Use this workflow to assess legal risk using a structured severity-by-likelihood framework, document the rationale, and recommend escalation paths.

Important: This framework is a decision-support tool, not legal advice. Teams should adapt it to their own risk tolerance and industry context.

## Risk Matrix

### Severity

| Level | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Negligible | Minor inconvenience and no material impact |
| 2 | Low | Limited impact and minor exposure |
| 3 | Moderate | Meaningful financial or operational impact |
| 4 | High | Significant impact and likely scrutiny |
| 5 | Critical | Severe business, reputational, or regulatory impact |

### Likelihood

| Level | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Remote | Highly unlikely |
| 2 | Unlikely | Could occur but not expected |
| 3 | Possible | Foreseeable under some conditions |
| 4 | Likely | Probable in similar situations |
| 5 | Almost Certain | Expected or imminent |

### Score

`Risk Score = Severity x Likelihood`

| Score | Level | Color |
|---|---|---|
| 1-4 | Low Risk | GREEN |
| 5-9 | Medium Risk | YELLOW |
| 10-15 | High Risk | ORANGE |
| 16-25 | Critical Risk | RED |

## Recommended Actions by Level

### GREEN

- Accept and document
- Track in the normal review cycle
- No formal escalation required

### YELLOW

- Mitigate and assign an owner
- Monitor actively
- Document triggers that would increase the score
- Brief relevant stakeholders

### ORANGE

- Escalate to senior counsel
- Build a mitigation plan
- Brief leadership
- Consider outside counsel
- Define contingency actions

### RED

- Escalate immediately
- Engage outside counsel
- Consider insurance notice, preservation, and crisis management
- Review at least daily until stabilized

## Documentation Standard

Use this structure for a formal assessment:

```markdown
## Legal Risk Assessment

**Date**: [date]
**Assessor**: [name]
**Matter**: [description]
**Privileged**: [Yes / No]

### 1. Risk Description
[description]

### 2. Background and Context
[context]

### 3. Risk Analysis
#### Severity Assessment: [1-5] - [Label]
[rationale]

#### Likelihood Assessment: [1-5] - [Label]
[rationale]

#### Risk Score: [score] - [GREEN / YELLOW / ORANGE / RED]

### 4. Contributing Factors
[what increases risk]

### 5. Mitigating Factors
[what reduces risk]

### 6. Mitigation Options
| Option | Effectiveness | Cost/Effort | Recommended? |
|---|---|---|---|
| [option] | High / Med / Low | High / Med / Low | Yes / No |

### 7. Recommended Approach
[recommended path]

### 8. Residual Risk
[expected score after mitigation]

### 9. Monitoring Plan
[cadence and triggers]

### 10. Next Steps
1. [action - owner - deadline]
2. [action - owner - deadline]
```

## Risk Register Format

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Risk ID | Unique identifier |
| Date Identified | Initial date |
| Description | Short summary |
| Category | Contract, Regulatory, Litigation, IP, Data Privacy, Employment, Corporate, Other |
| Severity | 1 to 5 |
| Likelihood | 1 to 5 |
| Risk Score | Calculated score |
| Risk Level | GREEN / YELLOW / ORANGE / RED |
| Owner | Responsible person |
| Mitigations | Current controls |
| Status | Open / Mitigated / Accepted / Closed |
| Review Date | Next review |
| Notes | Supporting context |

## Outside Counsel Triggers

Mandatory or near-mandatory engagement:

- Active litigation
- Government investigation
- Criminal exposure
- Securities disclosure implications
- Board-level matters

Strongly recommended:

- Novel legal issue
- Jurisdictional complexity
- Material financial exposure
- Specialized expertise gap
- Significant regulatory change
- M&A transaction work

Often appropriate:

- Complex contract disputes
- Sensitive employment matters
- Significant data incidents
- Material IP disputes
- Insurance coverage disputes

## Selection Factors for Outside Counsel

Consider:

- Subject-matter expertise
- Jurisdictional fit
- Industry knowledge
- Conflict clearance
- Budget and fee structure
- Existing panel relationships
