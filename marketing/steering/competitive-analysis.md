# Competitive Analysis

Research competitors and produce a structured positioning, messaging, and content comparison.

## Inputs

Gather:

1. Competitor names
2. Your company or product context
3. Focus areas such as messaging, features, content, pricing, or market presence

## Research Scope

Use primary and secondary sources where available:

- competitor websites
- news and press releases
- blogs and resource centers
- social profiles
- reviews and comparisons
- analyst mentions
- job postings
- SEO and traffic tools when connected

## Brief Structure

### 1. Executive Summary

- landscape overview
- biggest opportunity
- biggest threat

### 2. Competitor Profiles

For each competitor include:

- company overview
- messaging analysis
- product positioning
- content strategy
- strengths
- weaknesses

### 3. Messaging Comparison Matrix

Compare tagline, buyer, differentiator, tone, and core value proposition.

### 4. Content Gap Analysis

- topics they cover that you do not
- topics you own that they do not
- missing formats
- keyword opportunities

### 5. Opportunities

- positioning gaps
- messaging angles
- underserved audiences
- channel or content opportunities

### 6. Threats

- areas of competitor strength
- market shifts favoring them
- recent moves that matter

### 7. Recommended Actions

- quick wins
- longer-term strategic moves

## Analysis Rules

- Compare how each competitor frames the problem, promise, proof, and uniqueness.
- Focus on customer-facing positioning, not feature lists alone.
- Prefer actionable differentiation over generic “win themes.”
