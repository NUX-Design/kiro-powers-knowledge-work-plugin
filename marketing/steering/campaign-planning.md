# Campaign Planning

Generate a complete campaign brief with objectives, messaging, channel strategy, calendar, and success metrics.

## Inputs

Gather:

1. Campaign goal
2. Target audience
3. Timeline
4. Budget range
5. Optional context such as product, differentiators, geography, or prior learnings

## Campaign Brief Structure

### 1. Overview

- campaign name
- one-line summary
- primary and secondary objectives

### 2. Audience

- primary and secondary segments
- pain points and motivations
- channel and community habits
- buying stage

### 3. Key Messages

- core campaign message
- 3 to 4 supporting messages
- proof points
- channel-specific variations if needed

### 4. Channel Strategy

Recommend channels across owned, earned, and paid based on fit, effort, and budget.

### 5. Content Calendar

Provide a week-by-week or day-by-day plan:

```markdown
| Week | Content Piece | Channel | Owner/Notes | Status |
|------|--------------|---------|-------------|--------|
```

### 6. Content Asset List

- asset type
- purpose
- priority
- timeline

### 7. Success Metrics

- primary KPI with target
- secondary KPIs
- tracking method
- reporting cadence

### 8. Budget Allocation

If budget exists, split production versus distribution and recommend contingency.

### 9. Risks And Mitigations

- likely risks
- mitigations

### 10. Next Steps

- immediate actions
- approvals
- decision points

## Planning Rules

- Start from the objective, not the channel.
- Choose channels based on audience behavior.
- Work backward from milestones and launch dates.
- Leave room for iterative testing and reactive content.
