# Content Drafting

Draft marketing content tailored to a specific format, audience, and brand voice.

## Required Inputs

Gather before drafting:

1. Content type
2. Topic
3. Target audience
4. Key messages
5. Tone
6. Length or format constraints

If brand voice settings exist, apply them automatically. Otherwise ask for guidelines or use a neutral professional tone.

## Supported Content Types

### Blog Post

- 2 to 3 headline options
- strong introductory hook
- 3 to 5 sections with descriptive subheadings
- clear conclusion and CTA
- primary keyword suggestion and placement guidance

### Social Media Post

- platform-specific structure and length
- hook in the first line
- CTA or engagement prompt
- hashtag suggestions
- tone and formatting appropriate to LinkedIn, X, Instagram, or Facebook

### Email Newsletter

- 2 to 3 subject line options
- preview text
- greeting and body structure
- CTA text
- sign-off

### Landing Page

- headline and subheadline
- hero copy
- value propositions
- proof placeholders
- primary and secondary CTAs
- FAQ suggestions
- meta title and description suggestions

### Press Release

- proper headline and dateline structure
- lead paragraph covering who, what, when, where, why
- quotes and boilerplate placeholders

### Case Study

- results-oriented title
- customer context
- challenge, solution, and results
- metric prompts
- testimonial placeholder

## Output

After the draft, include:

- what brand voice or tone was applied
- SEO recommendations for web content
- suggested next steps or revision options
