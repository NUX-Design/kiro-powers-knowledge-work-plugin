# Content Frameworks

Reference structures, writing patterns, SEO fundamentals, and CTA guidance for marketing content across channels.

## Content Templates

- Blog posts: headline, hook, 3 to 5 body sections, conclusion, meta description
- Social posts: hook, body, CTA, hashtags
- Email newsletters: subject, preview text, hero, sections, primary CTA
- Landing pages: headline, subheadline, hero, value props, proof, objections, final CTA
- Press releases: headline, dateline, lead, body, boilerplate, media contact
- Case studies: title, snapshot, challenge, solution, results, quote, CTA

## Writing Best Practices

- Keep paragraphs short and scannable.
- Use active voice.
- Front-load the most important information.
- Match format and tone to the channel.
- Lead with benefits, not features, on conversion-oriented pages.

## SEO Fundamentals

- Choose one primary keyword and 2 to 3 supporting terms.
- Place the main keyword in the title, first paragraph, and one subheading.
- Keep title tags under roughly 60 characters and meta descriptions under roughly 160.
- Use descriptive URLs, alt text, and internal links.
- Structure content to answer common follow-up questions.

## Headline And Hook Formulas

Use repeatable patterns such as:

- How to [result] without [obstacle]
- [Number] ways to [result]
- Why [belief] is wrong
- The complete guide to [topic]
- [Do this], not [that]

Open with:

- a statistic
- a contrarian point
- a question
- a scenario
- a bold claim
- a short story

## CTA Principles

- Use clear action verbs.
- Make the next step specific.
- Add urgency only when genuine.
- Keep one primary CTA per asset when possible.
- Place CTAs where the reader has enough context to act.
