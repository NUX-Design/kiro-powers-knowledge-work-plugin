# Competitive Brief

Use this workflow to build a competitor analysis that informs product strategy, positioning, prioritization, or sales enablement.

## Inputs

Ask for:

- Specific competitor or competitors
- Feature area or focus
- Decision context such as strategy, sales, investor material, or prioritization

## Research Areas

Look across:

- Product and pricing pages
- Launch posts and changelogs
- Press and analyst coverage
- Customer reviews
- Job postings
- Community discussion
- Internal competitive docs, win/loss notes, or chat context when connected

## Competitive Set

Consider:

- Direct competitors
- Indirect competitors
- Adjacent competitors
- Substitute solutions

## Core Sections

### Competitor Overview

For each competitor include:

- Company summary
- Product positioning
- Recent momentum

### Feature Comparison

Use a consistent rating scale:

- Strong
- Adequate
- Weak
- Absent

### Positioning Analysis

Capture:

- Target customer
- Category claim
- Differentiator
- Value proposition
- Proof points

### Strengths and Weaknesses

Be evidence-based and honest.

### Opportunities and Threats

Surface:

- Gaps to exploit
- Areas requiring parity
- Strategic threats
- Market shifts worth monitoring

### Strategic Implications

Translate the analysis into:

- What to build or accelerate
- Where to differentiate
- What to deprioritize
- What to monitor

## Output Format

```markdown
## Competitive Brief

### Scope
- **Competitors:** [list]
- **Focus:** [area]
- **Decision Context:** [context]

### Competitor Overview
#### [Competitor]
- [summary]

### Feature Comparison
| Capability | Us | Competitor A | Competitor B |
|---|---|---|---|
| [capability] | Strong | Adequate | Weak |

### Positioning Analysis
- [competitor]: [positioning summary]

### Strengths and Weaknesses
- [competitor]: [strengths / weaknesses]

### Opportunities
- [opportunity]

### Threats
- [threat]

### Strategic Implications
1. [action]
2. [action]
```

## Final Rules

- Do not dismiss competitors casually.
- Focus on what matters to buyers.
- Separate signal from hype.
- Date the analysis mentally; competitive work ages quickly.
