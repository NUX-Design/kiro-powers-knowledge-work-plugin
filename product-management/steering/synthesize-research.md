# Synthesize Research

Use this workflow to turn raw interviews, surveys, tickets, analytics, and notes into findings, opportunity areas, and recommendations.

## Inputs

Accept:

- Pasted notes or transcripts
- Uploaded files
- Research docs from a knowledge base
- Tickets from feedback systems
- Analytics snapshots
- Meeting transcripts

Also ask:

- What type of research is this?
- How many sources or participants?
- What question or hypothesis is being investigated?
- What decision should the research inform?

## Processing Rules

For each source, extract:

- Key observations
- Quotes
- Behaviors
- Pain points
- Positive signals
- User context

Distinguish clearly between what users say and what users do.

## Theme Development

Use thematic analysis:

1. Read the data
2. Code observations
3. Group codes into themes
4. Review themes against the evidence
5. Refine theme names and meaning

Also use affinity mapping and triangulation when helpful.

## Prioritization Matrix

Classify findings across:

- High frequency + high impact
- Low frequency + high impact
- High frequency + low impact
- Low frequency + low impact

## Output Format

```markdown
## Research Synthesis

### Research Overview
- **Methods:** [interviews, survey, analytics, etc.]
- **Sources / Participants:** [count]
- **Question:** [research question]
- **Timeframe:** [dates]

### Key Findings
#### [Finding Title]
- **Finding:** [clear insight statement]
- **Evidence:** [quotes, data, observations]
- **Frequency:** [how many sources]
- **Impact:** [high / medium / low]
- **Confidence:** [high / medium / low]

### User Segments / Personas
- [segment]: [description, goals, pain points]

### Opportunity Areas
- [opportunity]

### Recommendations
1. [action]
2. [action]

### Open Questions
- [gap or follow-up]
```

## Persona Rules

Personas should emerge from evidence, not guesswork. Prefer behavioral distinctions over demographic ones.

## Recommendation Rules

- Tie each recommendation back to a finding.
- Be specific enough to act on.
- Flag confidence honestly.
- If sources conflict, report that rather than forcing consensus.
