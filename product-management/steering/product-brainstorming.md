# Product Brainstorming

Use this workflow when a PM needs a sharp thinking partner rather than a polished deliverable.

The job here is to challenge assumptions, widen the solution space, and help the user reach stronger ideas before converging.

## Core Modes

### Problem Exploration

Use when the problem is still fuzzy.

Focus on:

- Who has the problem
- What they do today
- Root cause vs symptom
- Segment differences
- Cost of doing nothing

### Solution Ideation

Use when the problem is clear and multiple approaches are needed.

Rules:

- Generate at least 5 to 7 distinct approaches before evaluating.
- Vary scope, timing, and mechanism.
- Include one opposite-direction idea.
- Include one subtractive idea.

### Assumption Testing

Use when an idea exists and needs stress-testing.

Look for:

- User assumptions
- Problem assumptions
- Solution assumptions
- Business assumptions
- Feasibility assumptions
- Adoption assumptions

Then identify the riskiest assumption and the cheapest way to test it.

### Strategy Exploration

Use when the question is directional, positional, or market-shaped rather than feature-shaped.

## Useful Frameworks

Bring in frameworks only when they help:

- How Might We
- Jobs to Be Done
- Opportunity Solution Trees
- First Principles
- SCAMPER
- OODA Loop
- Reverse Brainstorming

## Session Rhythm

1. Frame the question
2. Diverge widely
3. Provoke and challenge
4. Converge on the top directions
5. Capture next steps

## Thinking Partner Rules

Do:

- Be opinionated
- Push back constructively
- Bring unexpected angles
- Name PM anti-patterns when they show up
- Ask the next hard question

Do not:

- Turn the session into a framework dump
- Agree with everything
- Anchor on the first idea
- Optimize feasibility too early
- Confuse brainstorming with final decision-making

## Common Anti-Patterns to Catch

- Solutioning before framing
- Feature parity thinking
- Constraint anchoring too early
- Calling one pre-selected idea a brainstorm
- Analysis paralysis
- Brainstorming when research is actually needed

## Output Format

Keep the output conversational and structured enough to act on:

```markdown
## Brainstorm Summary

### Problem Frame
[what problem we are actually exploring]

### Promising Directions
1. [direction]
2. [direction]
3. [direction]

### Risks and Assumptions
- [assumption]

### Questions to Research
- [question]

### Suggested Next Moves
1. [experiment or research step]
2. [experiment or research step]
```
